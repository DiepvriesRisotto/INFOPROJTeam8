Leitner system

To practice the Leitner system, start by creating 10 vocabulary flashcards, writing the word on one side and the definition on the other. Label three boxes as 1, 2, and 3. Place all flashcards in Box 1. In your first study session, review each card in Box 1 and try to recall the definition. If you answer correctly, move the card to Box 2; if incorrect, leave it in Box 1. Follow a daily routine: on Day 1, review all cards in Box 1, moving correct answers to Box 2. On Day 2, review cards in Box 1 first, then Box 2, moving correct answers to Box 3 and incorrect ones back to Box 1. On Day 3, review cards in Box 1 and Box 3, skipping Box 2, with correct answers staying in Box 3 and incorrect ones moving to Box 1. Continue this cycle, reviewing Box 1 daily, Box 2 every other day, and Box 3 every fourth day, until all flashcards reach Box 3 and are answered correctly multiple times, indicating mastery of the vocabulary set.

Example vocabulary words include: Abate (to lessen in intensity), Benevolent (well-meaning and kindly), Cacophony (a harsh mixture of sounds), Dauntless (showing fearlessness), Ephemeral (lasting a short time), Frugal (economical), Garrulous (excessively talkative), Hubris (excessive pride), Incongruous (not in harmony), and Juxtapose (to place closely for contrast). Using this method will enhance your retention through spaced repetition.

Exercise

Write down your experiences doing the above exercise. How did it go at first? Did you get better? Did you find it helpfull? Try to track your progress doing the above exercise.
